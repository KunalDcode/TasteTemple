Dal Makhani

Here's a recipe for Dal Makhani using the Modern Pressure Cooker Method (approximately 2-3 hours):

prep time

Few Hours

cook time

2 hours.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Dal makhani lunch dinner food Indian food

instructions

Pressure Cook the Lentils:

In a pressure cooker, combine rinsed urad dal and rajma (if soaked, discard the soaking water) with 3 cups of water. Add the bay leaf, cardamom pod, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Pressure cook on medium heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the lentils and beans are softened. Let the pressure release naturally for 10-15 minutes, then release any remaining pressure manually.

Prepare the Masala:

While the lentils cook, heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute until fragrant. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices, about 10 minutes.

Spice Up Your Dal:

Add the Kashmiri red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and garam masala powder to the tomato mixture. Stir and cook for another minute to let the spices bloom.

Combine and Simmer:

Add the cooked lentils and beans (along with their cooking liquid) to the pan with the masala. Mash some of the lentils with a spoon to create a thicker consistency (optional). Season with salt to taste. Simmer for 15-30 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to meld.

Finishing Touches:

(Optional) For a richer texture, stir in a tablespoon of heavy cream or cashew paste (blended with a little water) just before serving. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro.

Serve and Enjoy:

Serve Dal Makhani hot with rice, naan, or roti.

ingredients

1 cup whole urad dal (black gram), rinsed (soak for a few hours if desired)

¼ cup rajma (red kidney beans), rinsed (soak for a few hours if desired)

1 tablespoon ghee or vegetable oil

1 bay leaf

1 black cardamom pod

2 cloves

1 inch cinnamon stick

1 medium onion, finely chopped

2 large tomatoes, finely chopped

1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste

1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder

½ teaspoon coriander powder

½ teaspoon cumin powder

½ teaspoon garam masala powder

Salt to taste

1 tablespoon heavy cream or cashew paste (optional)

Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

tools & Utensils

Large Bowl

Pressure Cooker

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups

Pan(medium/large)

Cutting Board

Immersion Blender

Plates or bowls ( for serving)
